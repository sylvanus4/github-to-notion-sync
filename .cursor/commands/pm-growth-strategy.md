# PM Growth Strategy

Design sustainable growth mechanisms by evaluating 5 growth loop types and 7 GTM motions, then producing a 90-day growth plan. References pm-go-to-market skill with growth-loops and gtm-motions sub-skills.

## Usage

```
/pm-growth-strategy Design growth loops for our B2B analytics SaaS targeting SMBs
/pm-growth-strategy 우리 마켓플레이스의 성장 루프와 GTM 모션 설계
```

## Workflow

### Step 1: Load skill and references

Read the `pm-go-to-market` skill (`.cursor/skills/pm-go-to-market/SKILL.md`) and its references:

- `references/growth-loops.md` — 5 loop types: viral, usage-driven, collaboration, UGC, referral
- `references/gtm-motions.md` — 7 motions: inbound, outbound, paid, community, partner, ABM, PLG

### Step 2: Evaluate growth loops

For the product/segment provided:

- Map applicable loop types (viral, usage, collaboration, UGC, referral)
- Assess feasibility, scalability, and time-to-value per loop
- Recommend primary and secondary loops with rationale
- Describe loop mechanics (input → action → output → amplification)

### Step 3: Assess GTM motions

Evaluate the 7 GTM motions against the business context:

- Inbound (content, SEO, demand gen)
- Outbound (sales-led, SDR)
- Paid (ads, sponsorships)
- Community (developer, user communities)
- Partner (channel, co-sell)
- ABM (account-based marketing)
- PLG (product-led growth)

Score each motion for fit, cost efficiency, and priority. Recommend 2–3 primary motions.

### Step 4: Produce 90-day growth plan

Synthesize into a 90-day plan with:

- Month 1: Loop prioritization, motion activation, quick wins
- Month 2: Loop optimization, channel scaling, experimentation
- Month 3: Consolidation, measurement, iteration roadmap

Include success metrics and decision checkpoints.

### Step 5: Output

Deliver structured markdown: loop evaluation table, GTM motion assessment, 90-day plan, and key metrics.

## Notes

- Request product description, target segment, and current traction if missing.
- PLG suits product-first businesses; sales-led motions for high ACV enterprise.
- Combine complementary loops (e.g., usage-driven + referral) for compound growth.
