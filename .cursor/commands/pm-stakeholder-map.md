# PM Stakeholder Map

Map stakeholders on a Power × Interest grid, then create tailored communication plans per quadrant. Include potential conflicts, escalation paths, and a RACI matrix. Uses pm-execution skill, stakeholder-map sub-skill.

## Usage
```
/pm-stakeholder-map Map stakeholders for the Q2 platform migration
/pm-stakeholder-map 이 프로젝트의 이해관계자 맵 만들어줘
/pm-stakeholder-map Who to engage for the pricing change initiative
/pm-stakeholder-map 가격 변경 이니셔티브 이해관계자 매핑
```

## Workflow

### Step 1: Identify Stakeholders
- List all individuals/groups affected by or affecting the initiative
- Include: exec sponsors, cross-functional leads, customers, partners, ops/support

### Step 2: Power × Interest Grid
- **Power**: Ability to influence outcomes (high/low)
- **Interest**: Level of concern or involvement (high/low)
- Plot each stakeholder in one of four quadrants:
  - High Power, High Interest: Manage closely
  - High Power, Low Interest: Keep satisfied
  - Low Power, High Interest: Keep informed
  - Low Power, Low Interest: Monitor

### Step 3: Communication Plan
- For each quadrant, define: frequency, channel, content type, owner
- Tailor message depth and format (exec summary vs detailed deck)

### Step 4: Conflicts and Escalation
- Identify potential conflicts between stakeholders (competing priorities, resource contention)
- Document escalation path: who resolves what, when to escalate to next level

### Step 5: RACI Matrix
- For key activities/phases: R (Responsible), A (Accountable), C (Consulted), I (Informed)
- Ensure single A per activity; avoid RACI-by-committee

## Notes
- Power and interest can shift — revisit map at phase gates
- "Keep satisfied" stakeholders are often execs; one poorly handled update can derail a project
- RACI: keep C and I lists lean to avoid meeting overload
