# PM Plan Launch

Build a complete GTM (go-to-market) strategy: beachhead segment, ideal customer profile (ICP), messaging, channels, and launch timeline. Multi-step pipeline using pm-go-to-market skill with beachhead-segment, ideal-customer-profile, gtm-strategy sub-skills.

## Workflow

### Step 1: Beachhead Segment
- Identify the narrowest viable segment to win first
- Criteria: accessible, underserved, strategic (expansion potential)
- Define: who they are, what job they hire the product for, why now
- Avoid "everyone" — constrain to one beachhead

### Step 2: Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)
- Firmographics: company size, industry, geography
- Technographics: current stack, buying process
- Champion profile: role, title, goals, challenges
- Scoring: what makes a prospect "ideal" (fit + readiness)

### Step 3: Messaging
- Value proposition: one sentence that resonates with beachhead
- Key messages: 3–5 proof points or differentiators
- Objection handlers: common concerns and responses
- Tailor by persona (buyer vs user vs champion)

### Step 4: Channels
- Acquisition: where does the beachhead discover and evaluate solutions?
- Prioritize: high intent channels (search, communities) vs broad reach (content, events)
- Channel mix: paid, organic, partnerships, outbound
- Budget/timeline constraints if provided

### Step 5: Launch Timeline
- Phases: pre-launch (beta, waitlist), launch (GA, PR, campaigns), post-launch (optimization)
- Key milestones: beta start, GA date, first paid customer target
- Dependencies: product readiness, marketing assets, sales enablement

### Step 6: Synthesis
- One-page GTM summary: beachhead, ICP, core message, primary channel, timeline
- Success metrics: awareness, pipeline, conversion, retention
- Risks and mitigation

## Notes
- Beachhead should be narrow enough to dominate, broad enough to matter
- ICP: qualify both "who" and "when" (buying triggers)
- Messaging: test with real prospects before scaling spend
