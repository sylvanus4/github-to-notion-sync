# Goose Competitor Teardown

Conduct a structured teardown of a competitor's entire go-to-market strategy — messaging, positioning, pricing, content, SEO, paid ads, sales process, and product differentiation. Produces a comprehensive competitive intelligence document with specific implications for your strategy.

Adapted from [gooseworks-ai/goose-skills](https://github.com/gooseworks-ai/goose-skills) composites/competitor-teardown.

## When to Use

- "Do a full teardown of [competitor]"
- "Analyze [competitor]'s GTM strategy"
- "경쟁사 분석 리포트", "경쟁사 전략 분해"
- "How does [competitor] position themselves?"

## Do NOT Use

- For competitive pricing comparison only (use goose-competitive-pricing-intel)
- For quick battlecard creation (use goose-battlecard-generator)
- For review-based competitive intelligence (use goose-review-intelligence-digest)

## Methodology

### Dimension 1: Messaging & Positioning
- Homepage hero message — what problem do they lead with?
- Tagline and value proposition
- Target audience (who do they think their ICP is?)
- Key differentiators they claim
- Language and tone (technical, enterprise, casual?)

### Dimension 2: Product & Pricing
- Core product capabilities
- Pricing model (per seat, usage, flat rate)
- Tier structure and feature gating
- Free trial or freemium offering
- Enterprise/custom pricing signals

### Dimension 3: Content & SEO
- Blog topics and frequency
- Top-ranking content pieces
- Content types (blog, guides, tools, templates)
- SEO footprint (estimated organic traffic, top keywords)
- Link building strategy signals

### Dimension 4: Paid Acquisition
- Active ad platforms (Google, LinkedIn, Facebook)
- Ad messaging and creative themes
- Landing page strategies
- Estimated ad spend range (if detectable)

### Dimension 5: Sales Process
- Sales-led vs product-led vs hybrid
- Demo availability and friction
- Sales outreach patterns (cold email, LinkedIn)
- Case studies and social proof usage

### Dimension 6: Strengths & Vulnerabilities
- Top 3 strengths (what they do well)
- Top 3 vulnerabilities (where they're weak)
- Opportunities for differentiation
- Threats if they improve

## Output: Comprehensive Competitor Teardown Report with 6 dimensions, specific implications, and actionable recommendations for your strategy
