# Voice Constant, Tone Flexes

The core mental model for brand voice enforcement: voice is constant, tone flexes.

## The Model

**Voice** = the brand's personality. It never changes regardless of content type, audience, or channel.
**Tone** = the emotional register that adapts to context while staying within the voice.

Think of it like a person: your personality stays the same whether you're presenting to a board or texting a friend, but your tone shifts.

## Voice Constants: "We Are / We Are Not"

Define the brand's personality as a two-column table of opposing attributes:

| We Are | We Are Not |
|--------|------------|
| Direct and clear | Vague or wishy-washy |
| Confident, not arrogant | Boastful or self-congratulatory |
| Warm and human | Robotic or corporate |
| Expert but accessible | Jargon-heavy or exclusionary |
| Honest about limitations | Overpromising or hedging |
| Action-oriented | Passive or abstract |

### How to Use the Table

- Every piece of content must align with the "We Are" column
- If content drifts toward a "We Are Not" attribute, flag and revise
- When in doubt, read the content aloud: does it sound like the "We Are" column or the "We Are Not" column?

## Voice Attributes (Persistent)

Define 3-5 core voice attributes that apply to ALL content:

| Attribute | Definition | In Practice |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| **Clarity** | Say what we mean, simply | Short sentences. Active voice. No hedge words. |
| **Confidence** | Own our expertise without arrogance | State positions directly. Use "we recommend" not "we think maybe" |
| **Warmth** | Sound human, not corporate | Use "you" and "your". Acknowledge the reader's context. |
| **Precision** | Be specific, not generic | Use numbers, names, concrete examples. Avoid "various" and "many". |

## Tone Flexes: The Context Matrix

Tone adapts across three dimensions:

### Dimension 1: Formality

| Level | When to Use | Signals |
|-------|------------|---------|
| **Formal** | Board presentations, legal communications, enterprise proposals | Full sentences, no contractions, structured format |
| **Professional** | Blog posts, email campaigns, customer communications | Contractions OK, conversational but polished |
| **Casual** | Social media, internal Slack, community forums | Sentence fragments OK, emoji acceptable, personality forward |

### Dimension 2: Energy

| Level | When to Use | Signals |
|-------|------------|---------|
| **High** | Product launches, feature announcements, success stories | Exclamation points (sparingly), dynamic verbs, forward momentum |
| **Neutral** | Educational content, how-to guides, documentation | Even-keeled, informative, steady pace |
| **Measured** | Incident communications, bad news, sensitive topics | Calm, empathetic, no spin, facts-first |

### Dimension 3: Technical Depth

| Level | When to Use | Signals |
|-------|------------|---------|
| **Deep** | Developer docs, technical blog posts, architecture discussions | Technical terms used freely, code examples, assumes expertise |
| **Moderate** | Product pages, feature overviews, manager-level comms | Technical concepts explained, analogies used, acronyms defined |
| **Accessible** | Marketing landing pages, social media, executive summaries | No jargon, benefit-focused, plain language |

### Tone-by-Context Matrix

Map content types to tone settings:

| Content Type | Formality | Energy | Technical Depth |
|-------------|-----------|--------|-----------------|
| Blog post | Professional | Neutral-High | Moderate |
| Landing page | Professional | High | Accessible |
| Email campaign | Professional | Neutral | Moderate |
| Social media (LinkedIn) | Professional | High | Moderate |
| Social media (Twitter/X) | Casual | High | Accessible |
| Press release | Formal | Neutral | Accessible |
| Case study | Professional | Neutral | Moderate |
| Sales proposal | Formal | Neutral | Moderate |
| Customer email | Professional | Measured-Neutral | Moderate |
| Incident report | Formal | Measured | Moderate |
| Internal Slack | Casual | Neutral | Deep |
| Pitch deck | Professional | High | Accessible |

## Enforcement Checklist

When reviewing content against voice and tone:

1. Read the "We Are / We Are Not" table. Does the content feel like the "We Are" column?
2. Check each voice attribute. Can you point to specific phrases that demonstrate each one?
3. Identify the target tone (formality + energy + technical depth). Does the content match?
4. Look for violations: jargon where accessible is needed, casual where formal is required, robotic where warm is expected.
5. Check terminology: are approved terms used? Are prohibited terms avoided?
