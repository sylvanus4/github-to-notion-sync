# Before/After Brand Voice Enforcement Examples

Concrete examples showing how brand voice enforcement transforms generic content into on-brand content. Each example shows the original (before), the enforced version (after), and the brand choices made.

## Email Campaign

### Before (Generic AI Output)
```
Subject: Exciting News About Our Platform!

Dear Valued Customer,

We are thrilled to announce that we have launched several new features
that we believe will significantly enhance your experience with our
platform. These features include various improvements to the dashboard,
multiple enhancements to reporting capabilities, and some updates to
the API.

We hope you will find these changes beneficial. Please do not hesitate
to reach out if you have any questions.

Best regards,
The Team
```

### After (Voice-Enforced)
```
Subject: Three new features that save you 2 hours a week

Hi [Name],

We shipped three updates this week based on what you've been asking for:

1. *Dashboard filters* — Save and reuse custom views across your team
2. *Scheduled reports* — Set it once, get it in your inbox every Monday
3. *API batch endpoints* — Process up to 1,000 records in a single call

Each one is live now. No setup needed.

Questions? Reply to this email — it goes straight to our team.

[Name], [Role]
```

### Brand Choices Made
- **Clarity**: Replaced "various improvements" with specific features and benefits
- **Precision**: Added concrete detail ("2 hours a week", "1,000 records")
- **Warmth**: Changed "Dear Valued Customer" to first name; "reply to this email" instead of "do not hesitate to reach out"
- **Confidence**: Removed hedging ("we believe", "we hope")
- **Tone**: Professional formality, high energy, moderate technical depth

## LinkedIn Post

### Before (Generic)
```
We're excited to share that our company has been recognized as a leader
in the industry! This achievement reflects our team's hard work and
dedication to providing best-in-class solutions. We're grateful for our
amazing customers and look forward to continuing to innovate.
#innovation #leadership #technology
```

### After (Voice-Enforced)
```
Our customers shipped 47% faster last quarter using [Product].

That's not a benchmark. That's what 200+ teams actually measured.

Here's what changed:
→ Fewer context switches (unified workspace)
→ Faster reviews (AI-assisted suggestions)
→ Less waiting (async-first collaboration)

The best part? These teams didn't change their process.
They changed their tools.

Full breakdown in the comments.

#developer-tools #productivity
```

### Brand Choices Made
- **Confidence**: Led with a specific result instead of a self-congratulatory announcement
- **Precision**: "47% faster" and "200+ teams" instead of "best-in-class solutions"
- **Clarity**: Three concrete reasons instead of vague claims
- **Warmth**: Conversational structure, direct address
- **Tone**: Professional formality, high energy, accessible depth

## Sales Proposal Opening

### Before (Generic)
```
Thank you for the opportunity to present this proposal. Our company is
a leading provider of enterprise software solutions with over 10 years
of experience serving Fortune 500 companies. We offer a comprehensive
suite of tools designed to streamline your operations and drive
efficiency across your organization.
```

### After (Voice-Enforced)
```
Your engineering team spends 11 hours a week on deployment coordination.
That's what your team leads told us in our discovery call on March 3rd.

This proposal shows how to cut that to under 2 hours — without changing
your CI/CD stack or retraining your team.

Here's what we'll cover:
1. The three bottlenecks we identified in your current workflow
2. How [Product] addresses each one (with your specific stack)
3. Implementation timeline: 3 weeks to full rollout
4. Pricing based on your 45-person engineering org
```

### Brand Choices Made
- **Clarity**: Opened with the customer's problem, not company credentials
- **Precision**: Used specific numbers from the discovery call
- **Confidence**: Made a direct claim ("cut that to under 2 hours") instead of vague promises
- **Warmth**: Referenced the actual conversation, showed we listened
- **Tone**: Formal, neutral energy, moderate technical depth

## Incident Communication

### Before (Generic)
```
We are currently experiencing some issues with our platform that may
be affecting some users. Our team is working diligently to resolve
the situation as quickly as possible. We apologize for any
inconvenience this may cause and will provide updates as they
become available.
```

### After (Voice-Enforced)
```
**Status: API latency elevated** (updated 2:15 PM ET)

**What's happening:** API response times are 3-5x slower than normal
for requests to the /analytics endpoints. Other endpoints are
unaffected.

**Who's affected:** Teams using the dashboard analytics or the
Analytics API directly. Roughly 15% of active accounts.

**What we're doing:** We identified the cause — a database index
was dropped during a migration at 1:45 PM ET. The rebuild is in
progress. Estimated resolution: 3:30 PM ET.

**What you can do now:** Cached dashboard views are still loading
normally. For API users, requests will succeed but may take
10-15 seconds instead of the usual 200ms.

Next update at 3:00 PM ET or sooner if resolved.
```

### Brand Choices Made
- **Clarity**: Structured with clear sections; no ambiguity about what's happening
- **Precision**: Specific endpoints, percentages, timestamps, and estimated resolution
- **Honesty**: Named the root cause directly instead of "some issues"
- **Warmth**: Provided actionable workaround; set clear expectations
- **Tone**: Formal, measured energy, moderate technical depth

## Content Type Quick Reference

| Content Type | Key Enforcement Focus |
|-------------|----------------------|
| Email | Replace hedge words, add specifics, use first name |
| Social media | Lead with results/data, cut self-congratulation |
| Sales proposal | Open with customer's problem, reference their context |
| Incident comms | Structure clearly, be precise about impact and timeline |
| Blog post | Use concrete examples, avoid "various" and "many" |
| Case study | Lead with measurable outcome, let customer speak |
| Press release | Facts first, quote adds personality, boilerplate is separate |
